Setting SMART Goals

You may or may not have heard about SMART goals. These are an extremely useful way of not only planning your goals, but also being accountable for them and tracking your progress. Setting SMART goals can help you use your time and resources wisely and productively, while bettering your chances of accomplishing them. The following guide will help you setting your goals:


1) Specific - If your goals are clear and specific, then you can focus all your efforts on it and be motivated to work towards them. Ask yourself the following:

  • What do I want to achive?
  • Who does it involve?
  • Why does this goal matter to me?

2) Measurable - By setting measurable goals, you can track your progress over time and stay focused You'll know exactly how far you have to go till you reach it. Ask yourself the following:

  • How much (time, money, resources etc.) will it take?
  • How will I know when I've accomplished it?

3) Achievable - In order to be successful, your goals should be realistic and attainable. Challenge yourself to go beyond, but remember that there are limits. There are some things that you don't have much control over, such as financial factors, but work towards achieving what you can. Ask yourself the following:

  • How realistic is the goal, considering constraint factors e.g. financials?
  • How can I achieve it?

4) Relevant - Make sure that your goal matters to you and that it's something you are willing to dedicate time and resources to. At the end of the day, no matter how many people are helping you, you are your own boss and you are responsible for achieving your goal. Ask yourself the following:

  • Does this seem worthwhile?
  • Will it make me happy?
  • Is now the right time for it?

5) Time-bound - When we get assignments, we get a deadline with it so that we know how much time we will have to finish it. Similarily, every goal needs a deadline, or a target time that we wish to complete it. Ask yourself the following:

  • How far will I be with it 5 weeks from now? 5 months?
  • What can I currently do for it?

We hope you can use SMART goals the next time you're planning out a goal!
